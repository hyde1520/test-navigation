Power of the Fire Feet-pound per second per feet2, meter-kilogram per second per meter2 The rate at which energy is released from a fire, calculated on a unit-area basis. This power is the source of energy that produces the convection column.  If the power of the fire is greater than the power of the wind, then the fire-wind system is dominated by the energy of the fire. Such fires are said to be plume dominated. Power of the fire is described by Rothermel (1991) based on work by Byram (1959).
Power of the Wind Feet-pound per second per feet2, meter-kilogram per second per meter2 The rate of kinetic energy in the wind field. It is expressed in the same units as the power of the fire.  If the power of the wind is greater than the power of the fire, then the fire-wind system is dominated by the energy of the wind.  Such fires are said to be wind-driven. Power of the wind is described by Rothermel (1991) based on work by Byram (1959).
Power Ratio A ratio The power of the fire divided by the power of the wind.  If the power ratio is less than one (i.e., the power of the wind is greater than the power of the fire), the fire is estimated to be wind-driven. If the power ratio is greater than one (i.e., the power of the fire is greater than the power of the wind), the fire is plume-dominated.
Wind-driven Fire Yes or No Yes indicates that it is a wind-driven fire (i.e., the power of the wind is greater than the power of the fire). No indicates that it is a plume-dominated fire (i.e., the power of the fire is greater than the power of the wind).

Safety Zone Size Output Parameters

Name Units Brief Description
Safety Zone Radius Feet, meters The radius of a circular safety zone just large enough to protect the specified number of personnel and heavy equipment from radiant heat injury. The calculation is based on Safety Zone Separation Distance plus the radius of a circle that fits all personnel and equipment. (Butler and Cohen, 1996; 1998a; 1998b)
Safety Zone Separation Distance Feet, meters The minimum distance a firefighter in protective clothing must be separated from flames to prevent radiant heat injury. Separation distance is calculated as 4 x flame heightThe average maximum vertical extension of flames at the leading edge of the fire front. Occasional flashes that rise above the general level of flames are not considered. This distance is less than the flame length if flames are tilted due to wind or slopeThe ratio of the elevational change (rise) over a horizontal distance (run).. (From http://www.nwcg.gov/pms/pubs/glossary/f.htm.). Flame length is used as a worst-case estimator of flame height. (Butler and Cohen, 1996; 1998a; 1998b)

Backing Spread Distance Chains, meters Based on the elliptical fire shape model, this parameter is the distance the fire travels in a specified period of time in the direction that is 180 degrees from the direction of maximum spread.
Fire Length Chains, meters The distance from the head of a fire to the back end of the fire calculated for an elliptically shaped fire. Fire length is the sum of the forward spread distance and the backing spread distance. (Anderson, 1983; Andrews, 1986)
Forward Spread Distance Chains, meters Based on the elliptical fire shape model, this parameter is the distance the head fire travels in a specified period of time. This measurement is the same as surface spread distance for a heading fire.
Length-to-Width Ratio A ratio The length-to-width ratio is a function of the effective wind speed. Fire burning under a high wind speed will be long and narrow with a large length-to-width ratio. Although this parameter applies to surface fires, crown fire length-to-width ratios can also be calculated. (Anderson, 1983; Andrews, 1986)

Note: The length-to-width ratio of an elliptically shaped fire is sometimes called length-to-breadth ratio.
Maximum Fire Width Chains, meters Based on the elliptical fire shape model, this parameter is the maximum width of the ellipse. (Anderson, 1983; Andrews, 1986)
Perimeter Chains, meters The perimeter of a point source fire without suppression action is based on the elliptical fire shape model. Perimeter is the distance around the outside of the ellipse. Although this parameter applies to surface fires, crown fire perimeters can also be calculated. (Anderson, 1983; Andrews, 1986)

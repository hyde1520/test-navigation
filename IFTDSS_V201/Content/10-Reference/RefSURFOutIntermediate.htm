Heat sink Btus per feet3, kilojoules per meter3 This parameter is used in computing the surface ROS. ROS is calculated from the ratio of the heat flux received from the source (heat source, which is the numerator) and the heat required for ignition by the potential fuel (heat sink, the denominator). The heat required for ignition is dependent upon ignition temperature, moisture content of the fuel, and the amount of fuel involved in the ignition process. This is an intermediate value in Rothermel's surface fire spread model (1972).
Heat source Btus per feet2 per minute, kilowatts per meter2 This parameter is used in computing the surface ROS. ROS is calculated from the ratio of the heat flux received from the source (heat source, which is the numerator) and the heat required for ignition by the potential fuel (heat sink, which is the denominator). The propagating heat flux is increased by wind and slope factors due to flame tilting over the potential fuel to calculate the total propagating heat flux or heat source. This is an intermediate value in Rothermel's surface fire ROS model (1972).

Packing Ratio A ratio The packing ratio (Beta) is the ratio of the fuel bed bulk density (lb / ft3) to the fuel particle density (lb / ft3). This is an intermediate value in Rothermel's surface fire ROS model (1972).
Relative Packing Ratio A ratio The ratio of the fuel bed packing ratio to the optimum packing ratio (Beta divided by Beta-op). This ratio is a factor in determining the optimum reaction velocity. This is an intermediate value in Rothermel's surface fire ROS model (1972).

The FCCS summarizes and calculates the following fuel characteristics:

  • Percentage cover. Percentage cover is generally a fuelbed input and not actually calculated. It refers to the percentage cover by crown projection of trees, shrubs, and nonwoody fuelsCombustable materials that a fire consumes in order to burn. The models in IFTDSS take fuel inputs such as trees, bushes, and grasslands., and by linear estimates of woody and ground fuels.
  • Depth. Depth of each fuel stratum and category is a basic input measure of the vertical structure of a fuelbed. The term height (ft) is used in canopy, shrub, and nonwoody fuels strata; depth (inches) is used for woody fuelsWoody fuels have a true woody stem. Woody fuels include sound wood, rotten wood, and stumps., LLMLitter, lichen, and moss. The top layer of forest or rangeland floors., and ground fuels strata. For consistency, FCCS only considers the depth of downed fine woody fuels less than or equal to three inches in diameter in the woody fuels stratum and does not include stumps or piles in this measure.
  • Fuel loading (total, live, and dead). Unless input directly into FCCS, fuel loadingDry weight in tons/acreA measure of land used in the Imperial and US systems; 43,560 square feet or 4,840 square yards. There are 640 acres in one square mile. One acre is 0.405 hectares. of fuels. is calculated from dimensional inputs and summarized as total, live, and dead loading by stratum, category, and subcategory in reports.
  • Fuel area index (FAI). Fuel area index (FAI) is a relatively new and useful concept in fire behavior modeling (Riccardi et al., 2007b). Analogous to leaf area index, FAI is the surface area of fuels per unit ground area. To assimilate the heterogeneous fuel structures captured by FCCS fuelbedsA fuelbed is an area of similar fuel characteristics within a unit. A fuelbed contains one or more fuelbed strata (e.g., canopy, shrubs, etc)., FCCS calculates the total fuel surface area on all size classes of fuels, including live foliar biomass. FAI is used in the calculation of surface fire behavior and FCCS fire potentials.
  • Packing ratio. Packing ratio is the fraction of a fuelbed volume that is occupied by fuel particles (Rothermel, 1972) and is an important factor in predicting fire behavior.
  • Density. Density refers to the number of trees, stumps, and other fuel categories per acre. Density values are input into the FCCS and summarized and reported by the calculator.
  • Height to live crown. Applicable only to the tree category of the canopy stratum, height to live crown, defined as the distance from the ground to bottom of each canopy layer, is input into the FCCS and summarized and reported by the calculator.
  • Diameter at breast height. Applicable only to the tree and snag categories of the canopy stratum, diameter at breast height is input into the FCCS and summarized and reported by the calculator.

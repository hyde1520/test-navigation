The rate of smoke production from fire varies by combustion phase. Less smoke is produced per ton of fuel consumed during the efficient flaming stage than during the less efficient smoldering phase (Peterson and Sandberg, 1988). Consequently, fuel consumptionThe amount of fuels by weight (tons/acreA measure of land used in the Imperial and US systems; 43,560 square feet or 4,840 square yards. Consume calculates emissions based on a set of emission factors based on the type of fuel and the combustion phase of the burn. This table lists emission factors by fuel type (activity or natural), vegetation (Douglas fir, mixed conifer, ponderosa pine, hardwood, juniper, sage brush), and combustion stageA unique burn environment composed of specific types of fuelbed strata. (flaming, smoldering, and residual smoldering) (Hardy et al., 2001; Ottmar and Baker, 2007). The emission factors reported in Consume are calculated using the flaming and smoldering emission factors from that table and weighted according to the amount of fuel consumed in each phase.

Step 2: Calculating emissions by combustion phase

Consume calculates pollutant emissions by combustion phase using the emission factors  by emisison factor group. In rare cases where an appropriate cover type is not available or is not listed for an FCCS fuelbed, Consume will use a default emissions factor (EFG #0).

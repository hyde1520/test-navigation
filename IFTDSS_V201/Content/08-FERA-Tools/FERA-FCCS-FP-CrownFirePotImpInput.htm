Input Variables Important to Crown Fire Potentials

Stratum CanopyThe stratum containing the crowns of the tallest vegetation present (living or dead), usually above 20 feet. (Source: NWCGNational Wildfire Coordinating Group. An operational group that coordinates programs of participating wildfire management agencies. Glossary of Wildland Fire Terminology.) Canopy characteristics (including canopy bulk density and canopy base height) for conifer stands are (1) Douglas-fir/lodgepole pine, (2) lodgepole pine, (3) Ponderosa pine/Douglas-fir, (4) Ponderosa pine, and (5) Sierra Nevada mixed conifer (Scott and Reinhardt, 2005).

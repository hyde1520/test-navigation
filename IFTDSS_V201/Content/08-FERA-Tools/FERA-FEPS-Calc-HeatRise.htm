Heat Rise

FEPSFire Emission Production Simulator. A FERAFire and Environmental Research Applications Team. A USDA Forest Service research team focusing on fuels and fire and landscape ecology. tool for modeling consumption, emissions, and heat release characteristics of prescribed burns and wildland fires. calculates heat rise using three methods. These methods are

  1. The maximum heat rise based on the work Briggs (1975).
  2. Empirical minimum heat rise values based on equations developed by David V. Sandberg (personal communication).
  3. Empirical maximum heat rise values based on equations developed by David V. Sandberg (personal communication).

Each of these sets of equations calculate the minimum and maximum heat rise for each hour of the event; the subscript i refers to a time step, or hour, of the fire burning period.

Modified Transport Wind Speed

The transport wind speed at hour i used in further heat release calculations is modified slightly from the values entered by the user. It is the maximum of the current transport wind entered in the hourly data grid, or twice the value entered for the previous hour. It is further limited to a minimum value of windspeed (Wmin).

Minimum Travel Time (MTT) Modeling Assessment Introduction:

The minimum travel time (MTT) methods allow the user to assess fire growth across a landscape. MTT can be more efficient than other fire spread models in computing fire growth. MTT is particularly well suited to short-range assessments of fire growth where weather conditions are held constant and/or where many simulations from multiple ignition sources are used, such as in fire-risk assessments. MTT is also well suited to assess the effects of fuelCombustable materials that a fire consumes in order to burn. The models in IFTDSS take fuel inputs such as trees, bushes, and grasslands. treatment optimization, where treatment units are being strategically located to impede fire spread through critical pathways on the landscape.

MTT produces many useful assessment output parameters.

Arrival Time: A representation of time (fraction of an hour) it takes the fire to reach a location on the landscape from an ignition source. You may think of this as time step perimeters.

Major Flow Paths: Displays the minimum time pathways as the fire moves across the landscape. Just as water flows in a path of least resistance, major flow paths displays the path across the landscape that fire would spread most rapidly.

The objective of a landscape-level hazard analysis is to identify areas within a landscape that could be hazardous if a fire were to occur. This analysis can help identify areas where fuels treatment may be warranted and help prioritize areas of potentially high fire hazardFor IFTDSS, a fire hazard is an act or phenomenon with the potential to do harm. It can be expressed as potential fire behavior (e.g., fire line intensity, crown scorch height), or a property of the fuels, such as fuel loading or vegetation biomass, or both. A fire hazard is independent of weather and describes the fuels at one point in time..

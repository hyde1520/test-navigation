We choose the Calkin et al. (2010a) approach that incorporates risk assessment research by Mark Finney and Alan Ager for our first set of risk assessment modules for several reasons. When This Approach Is Useful

The worst case approach is useful when a conservative estimate of fire hazard and risk is appropriate or when the user is interested in identifying high fire hazard situations during wind-driven wildfires.

In the future, this tool will also be useful for assessing the effectiveness of a fuel treatment at reducing the hazard of a fire burning as a head fire.

Overview

Prescribed burns are planned to meet management and operational objectives in accordance with The Interagency Prescribed Fire Planning and Implementation Procedures Guide (U. S. Department of Agriculture and U. S. Department of the Interior, 2014). All prescribed fires require an approved plan that must be followed when a burn is conducted. The prescribed fire burn plan is the legal document that provides an agency administrator with the information needed to approve a prescribed fire. How IFTDSS Helps

Prescribed burn planning requires a burn planner to collect data, run fire behavior and fire effects simulations over a range of environmental variables, and make decisions that enable the burn plan objectives to be met during the process of maintaining control of the fire. To complete these tasks, prescribed burn planners typically use a variety of software tools with various data requirements.

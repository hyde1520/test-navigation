Modeling Considerations

Models are based on simplified assumptions, such as uniform fuelCombustable materials that a fire consumes in order to burn. The models in IFTDSS take fuel inputs such as trees, bushes, and grasslands. and steady-state fire spread.

Rather than basing your burn plan prescription solely on modeling, the USDA and U.S. DOI (2014) allows for the use of empirical evidence (historical evidence or researched data) and judgment. These can be used to identify or corroborate prescriptions.

Weaknesses in modeling can be overridden, but must be justified with empirical evidence and/or verified actual fire behavior.

(USDA and U.S. DOI, 2014, p. 28)

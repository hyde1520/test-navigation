Improved Help Center

Version 3.1.1 Released Oct 29 - Nov 5 2018

  • More wind options in LFB - select options to use gridded winds, upslope winds, downslope winds, or static winds.
  • Fuel moisture conditioning - When running models, condition fuel moistures with IFTDSS data, or upload your own weather stream data.
  • AOIs for Reports - Now generate reports constrained by an area of interest for any report (Auto97th, LCP summary, Model summary).
  • Copy run button - Easily copy, edit, and re-run models.
  • Quickly view model run inputs - In My Modeling Playground, click the 'view input' button under any model output file to view a dialogue box displaying all model inputs used to create that file.
  • Improved Help Center - More easily browse and find Help Center topics.

Coming in 2019

Wildfire Risk Assessment - Under Development

A workflow and tools to perform a Quantitative Risk Assessment (QRA) from the project to the unit scale is under development in IFTDSS. The process follows the steps outlined in GTR-315, “A wildfire risk assessment framework for land and resource management.” IFTDSS is unique in that it will contain a comparison functionality allowing users to complete a QRA and then compare treatment alternatives to understand the potential impacts on risk.

General Timeline for Wildfire Risk Assessment Development in IFTDSS

The IFTDSS development team and technical advisors have already been working on the technical details of the Quantitative Risk Assessment through the summer of 2018. Continued work on integrating the Landscape burn probability model will continue through the Fall and Winter of 2018. Throughout 2019 the team will develop the user interface, workflow and data specifications within IFTDSS with portions of the workflow being incrementally available throughout the year.

Create Summary Reports

Summary reports outline landscape features and predicted worst-case fire behavior (97th percentile fuel moisture and wind). These high level landscape summaries are intended as a starting point for evaluating your area as part of the fuels management planning process.

Below, we discuss creating Summary reports from the Landscape Planning Cycle in IFTDSS. There is also a button that appears under selected landscapes in My Workspace labeled "Request a Report". That button only generates a summary of landscape features, not the additional Auto97th information that we discuss here from the Landscape Planning Cycle.

Navigate to Landscape Summary
  1. Go to the IFTDSS Planning Cycle from the top navigation menu
  2. Click the Landscape Evaluation Stage of the Cycle

  3. Click the Landscape Summary button.

Creating a Landscape Summary

Landscape Summary inputs and buttons are listed below: 

  1. Specify an existing landscape to summarize using the drop-down menu. If you do not have a landscape yet use the Create Here or Create in Map Studio buttons to create one. Creating a landscape in Map Studio will require you to navigate back the Landscape Summary interface when your landscape is created. Newly created landscapes may take a few minutes to generate. If you choose a newly created landscape that is still being created, it will have an hourglass icon displayed after the landscape name; use the Refresh button to the left of the drop-down field until the hourglass is replaced by a green checkmark , indicating your new landscape is ready.
  2. (Optional) Specify an area of interest (polygon or shapefile) within an existing landscape for summary. To run a summary across the entire landscape, leave this field blank.

  3. Click the Request a Report button.

  4. A prompt appears with the name of your landscape across the top. Click Create Report to generate the Summary Report.

  5. The icon indicates the Summary Report is being generated. You can refresh and check the status by clicking the refresh button .

  6. When finished your Summary Report will be available by clicking on the report name in the confirmation dialogue box.

    7. Depending upon landscape size, your summary may take a few minutes to complete.

It may also be viewed from My Workspace, in the same folder as the landscape you chose to summarize, as well as listed in Modeling Playground. You may also view your report outputs in Map Studio.

Viewing a Landscape Summary

The Automatic 97th Percentile (Auto97th) fire behavior model run, and subsequent summary report, uses the capabilities of FlamMap to model fire behavior based on the 97th percentile fire weather conditions for your landscape. The summary report displays landscape file attributes, and modeled fire behavior outputs (listed below) spatially, and by percent in a bar chart, pie chart, and as a table. At the bottom right of each fire behavior and landscape feature is an Export Options button that will allow you to print the summary for that feature, or download an image (in .png format) of the pie chart, bar chart, or table.

Landscape Features Summarized:

  • Fuel Model
  • Canopy Cover (percent)
  • Stand Height (meters)
  • Canopy Base Height (meters)
  • Canopy Bulk Density (kg/m3)
  • Aspect (degrees)
  • Slope (degrees)
  • Elevation (meters)

Modeled Fire Behavior Summarized:

  • Flame Length (meters)
  • Rate of Spread (m/min)
  • Intensity (kW/m)
  • Heat per Unit Area (kJ/m2)
  • Crown Fire Activity (No Fire, Surface Fire, Passive Crown Fire, and Active Crown Fire)

Fuel Moistures and RAWS Details Summarized

Fuel moisture and RAWS details are summarized in the header of the "Model Characteristics" section of the summary report and include:

  • 97th percentile fuel moistures for 1,10,100 hr fuels; live herbaceous fuels; live woody fuels
  • Station Name
  • Station Observation Start Date
  • Station Observation End Date
  • Station Elevation
  • Station Aspect
  • Station Latitude
  • Station Longitude
Saving & Printing a Landscape Summary

Saving

In addition to being saved in My Workspace, you may also save Landscape Summary reports to your local computer. Using the download button in the top right will download the entire report as a .pdf file. Clicking the options button to the right of each section of the report, will save that specific image or graph.

Printing from your internet browser

Summary reports can also be printed directly from the internet browser. To print the full summary using Google Chrome:

  1. Right click anywhere within the white space of summary, in this example, we've clicked to the right of the summary title. This brings up several options, including Print

  2. Click Print from the options and follow your normal routine for printing a document from your computer.

Technical Documentation on the Auto97th Landscape Summary

The Automatic 97th Percentile (Auto97th) fire behavior model run and summary are available from the Landscape Evaluation stage of the Planning Cycle, via the Landscape Summary task. The run, and subsequent summary report, uses the capabilities of FlamMap to model fire behavior based on the 97th percentile fire weather conditions for your landscape as determined by the Remote Automatic Weather Station (RAWS) closest to the center of your chosen Landscape.

While currently the selection of RAWS is automatic, in future releases of IFTDSS you will be able to specify the RAWS station of your choosing.

Inputs

Landscape

The landscape file contains all the information needed to complete landscape fire behavior modeling in IFTDSS. The landscape consists of geospatial layers characterizing ground, surface, and canopy fuels, as well as topography. The specific components of a landscape file include:

  • Fuel Model
  • Canopy Cover (percent)
  • Stand Height (meters)
  • Canopy Base Height (meters)
  • Canopy Bulk Density (kg/m3)
  • Aspect (degrees)
  • Slope (degrees)
  • Elevation (meters)

Crown Fire Method

For the Auto97th run, the crown fire calculation option is set to the Scott and Reinhardt (2001) method, but you may also choose the Finney (1998) method. Both methods are based on the same component crown fire behavior models, although there are minor differences in implementation. These subtle differences lead to significantly more predicted crown fire activity using the Scott and Reinhardt method compared to the Finney method.

Visit Crown Fire Calculations Options Topic for more details about these two options.

Weather

The Auto97th run use 97th percentile fire weather conditions. Percentiles are based on a scale of 0-100 and are used to sort and rank a collection of data. For wildfire, when values at the upper end of the scale occur, we would expect to see complex fires where initial attack may often fail. The 97th percentile is often termed “the worst-case scenario.”

To determine the 97th percentile conditions, IFTDSS chooses the closest Remote Automated Weather Station (RAWS) to generate fuel moistures, wind speed and wind direction. The RAWS is selected by:

  1. Identifying the center of the landscape extent.
  2. Calculating the distance from the center to the nearest RAWS using a horizontal/vertical (elevation) algorithm.
  3. Ranking the stations and selecting the station which is the “closest” and has weather data requisite for running the model.

The RAWS selected, and the fuel moisture and wind scenario for the automated Auto97th model, cannot be changed. It is intended to be a starting point to understand your landscape and predicted fire behavior. You may choose to run a Landscape Fire Behavior model run with user-input values in the Modeling Playground.

Once a RAWS station is selected, the Energy Release Components (ERCs) are calculated for every day. ERC is related to the available energy within the flaming front at the head of a fire. The ERC is considered a composite fuel moisture index as it reflects the contribution of all live and dead fuels to potential fire intensity. The ERC has memory, each daily calculation considers the past 7 days in calculating the new number making it relatively stable indicator of fuel conditions over time.

The 97th percentile fuel moistures are calculated for dead (1hr, 10 hr, and 100 hr) and live (herbaceous and woody) fuels by averaging the values for all days where ERC is at the 97th percentile.

To determine the 97th percentile wind speed and direction, the wind observations during the normal burning period (1200-2000 hours) for the peak fire season is summarized in a table. The peak fire season is determined by the month with the highest ERC, and the month immediately before and after it. The table includes the probability of wind speed and direction for set bins.

Outputs

Outputs are displayed on a map across the landscape, as a bar chart, pie chart, and in tabular form. The summary report will include both the inputs above and the following fire behavior outputs:

  • Flame Length (meters)
  • Rate of Spread (m/min)
  • Intensity (kW/m)
  • Heat per Unit Area (kJ/m2)
  • Crown Fire Activity (No Fire, Surface Fire, Passive Crown Fire, and Active Crown Fire)

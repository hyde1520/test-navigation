MTT Technical Documentation

Minimum travel time (MTT) Fire Spread is a two-dimensional fire growth model (Finney, 2002). MTT calculates fire growth and behavior by searching for the set of pathways with minimum fire spread times from point, line, or polygon ignition sources. In theory, the results are identical to wave-front expansion used in Farsite with the exception that all weather and fuel moisture conditions are held constant over time with MTT but are allowed to vary with time in Farsite.

Running MTT Fire Spread

To run MTT from the IFTDSS Modeling Playground:

  1. Select MTT Fire Spread from the Select Model to Run drop-down menu and click Create Run.

  2. Select a landscape from the drop-down list or create a new one using the Create Here button.

  3. Click on the drop-down heading for each input and fill out each as shown in the steps below.


  4. Select the type of wind you would like to represent, and input the windspeed in mph, and wind direction in degrees. See the wind assessment topic for more details on picking a wind type.

  5. Choose a crown fire method and fuel moisture.

    Input the fuel moistures for woody and herbaceous fuels. If needed, you may create additional rows to use a different fuel moisture for each fuel model on your landscape. Need help representing fuel moistures? See the fuel moisture estimation topic.

  6. Specify the ignition for your landscape from the drop-down. You may either choose a specific shape on your landscape, or simulate ignition in the center of your landscape.

  7. Enter the simulation time. You will enter a time length for the burn period, in hours, and the number of burn periods.

  8. Enter a spotting probability in percent.

  9. Give your run a descriptive name and click Run. When finished your run will be available in Modeling Playground and My Workspace.

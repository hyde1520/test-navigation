Can find a treatment or wildfire? Click on the appropriate drop-down below for information on how to proceed.

How do I find my treatments?

Treatments in FTEM are organized by wildfire, pick your wildfire first using the date list and wildfire name search. Once you have chosen your wildfire, click the Treatments button to see a list of the treatments interactions for that fire. Use the Add or Remove Treatment buttons to refine your treatment list.

What about my region? When you create your FTEM account you will be asked to specify a region. This does not limit the treatments or wildfires you see. Your region simply constrains the map extent you see when you first open FTEM. You are still free to scroll and search for fires/treatments anywhere on the FTEM map.

What if I don't see my wildfire?

Wildfire data is pulled into the FTEM system nightly from IRWIN and GEOMAC.

The most common reason a wildfire is not visible in FTEM is a lack of Control Date, Containment Date, or Out Date in the system of record.

  1. If this date remains blank, it will not become visible in FTEM until 45 days after the start date.
  2. For those familiar with WFDSS who have the proper permissions, they could update those date fields, and the wildfire would be pulled into FTEM the following day.
  3. Alternatively, an FTEM user could contact their FTEM Agency Administrator to let them know the fire is not appearing, and to check for this issue in the system of record.

For more details see the FTEM Wildfire Technical Topic.

What if I don't see my treatment?

Treatments are pulled into FTEM nightly from FACTS and NFPORS nightly and must have the following attributes in the system of record:

  • A completion date after January 1 2005
  • For FACTS data, it must be Hazardous Fuels Reduction treatment or non-NFP treatment (Keypoint 3 or 6)

If your treatment meets the above criteria but is still not visible in FTEM:

  1. Try increasing your search buffer for interacting treatments. This is done after you select your wildfire, but using the Add Treatments button on the Treatments tab. See the Search Buffer Topic for a step by step walk-though of how to do this.
  2. If the above criteria are met, and increasing the buffer area does not help, you may need to follow up with your FTEM Agency Administrator to have your treatment entered in the system of record. Treatments may take some time to become visible and individual agencies may upload records at different intervals.

For more details see the FTEM Treatments Technical Topic.

Step by Step Walkthrough

The Landscape Fire Behavior modeling option can be reached in IFTDSS via My Modeling Playground, or the Landscape Evaluation Stage of the Planning Cycle (which will take you to My Modeling Playground). Once the Landscape Fire Behavior model is chosen from My Modeling Playground, follow these steps to populate and run the model: 

Step 1: Give your run a descriptive name to help differentiate the run, the name may include a specific windspeed, fuel condition, or some other distinguishing characteristic.

Step 2: Choose a landscape for your run from the drop-down menu. You also have the option of :

creating a new landscape from within the run interface
  1. Use the bounding box fields and enter the coordinates, in degrees, for the west, east, south, and north bounds of your area.

    2. The maximum area for a landscape is 12,188,160 acres (138 miles x 138 miles).

  2. Choose the version of LANDFIRE data you would like to use from the drop-down list
  3. Choose the fire behavior fuel model (Fuel Model 40 or Fuel Model 13) to include in your landscape file from the drop-down list.
  4. Give your landscape a descriptive name, this will be helpful as you accumulate more landscape files in IFTDSS.
  5. Select the folder where you would like to store your landscape file, or click + Folder to create and name a new folder to place your landscape file.
  6. Click Create to generate your landscape file.
or creating a new landscape from within Map Studio

To create landscape from within Map Studio click the Landscape Tools button along the bottom of Map Studio. In the Landscape Tools box, make sure the Create new tab is selected.

In the Create new tab specify the requirements for your landscape:

  1. Use the Map Studio tools such as zoom, basemap layers, etc. to locate your desired area.
  2. Click the Select draw mode, mouse to your location on the map, hold down the left mouse key, and draw a rectangle around your desired area, releasing the button when you are finished.
  3. Alternatively, if you wish to enter exact coordinates rather than draw a box around your area, you may do so by entering the coordinates, in degrees, for the west, east, south, and north bounds of your desired area.
  4. Choose the version of LANDFIRE data you would like to use.
  5. Choose the fire behavior fuel model (Fuel Model 40 or Fuel Model 13) you would like included in your landscape file.
  6. Give your landscape a descriptive name, this will be helpful as you accumulate more landscape files in IFTDSS.
  7. Select the folder in which you would like to store your landscape file.
  8. Click Create to generate your landscape file.

After selecting Create, it may take a few minutes to generate your landscape. You may check its status by refreshing the Layer List in Map Studio.

If you have created a landscape in Map Studio using the Create in Map Studio button (for example, this is an option in Landscape Summary, and other tasks) you will need to navigate back to your task to continue that process using your newly created landscape.

The maximum area for a landscape is 12,188,160 acres (138 miles x 138 miles).

When your landscape is chosen, you will notice a box appears displaying details about that landscape, including, source data, boundaries, and acres.

All saved runs are stored in the same folder as your selected landscape, or in the ‘Playground folder’ if a new landscape is created but no folder specified.

Step 6: Upon completing all required inputs, you may choose to:

Save - which will save your inputs, but still provide a chance to edit and re-save before running the model.

Save and Run - which will save your inputs and run the model.

Step 3: Specify windspeed, in miles per hour, at 20 feet above the ground or above the vegetation (allowed values 0-100 mph).

Enter wind direction as the direction the wind is blowing from (in degrees clockwise from north, with north being 0 or 360 degrees). Alternatively you may enter -1 for upslope wind, or -2 for downslope wind.

Step 4: Specify a crown fire calculation method, and the foliar moisture content (in percent).

Step 5: Specify fuel moisture, in percent, for:

  • 1 hour fuels (1hr FM) - allowed values 2-100
  • 10 hour fuels (10hr FM) - allowed values 2-100
  • 100 hour fuels (100hr FM) - allowed values 2-100
  • Herbaceous fuels (Herb) - allowed values 30-250
  • Live Woody fuels (WoodyFM) - allowed values 60-200

If you would like to specify fuel moisture by fuel model, you may do so by adding rows, click + add row. This will add a new line. Using the drop down list, choose a fuel model, then specify the fuel moisture values. You may repeat this as many times as needed.

If you choose to only populate one row, those fuel moistures will be applied to all fuel models in your landscape.

For more guidance on choosing fuel moistures see the Estimating Fuel Moistures Topic.

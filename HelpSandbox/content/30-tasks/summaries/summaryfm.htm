Using Landscape Summary to Determine Fuel Moistures

Landscape summary reports outline landscape features and predicted worst-case fire behavior (97th percentile fuel moisture and wind). To use this summary to find fuel moistures, follow the steps below:

Creating a Landscape Summary

Landscape Summary inputs and buttons are listed below: 

  1. Specify an existing landscape to summarize using the drop-down menu. If you do not have a landscape yet use the Create Here or Create in Map Studio buttons to create one. Creating a landscape in Map Studio will require you to navigate back the Landscape Summary interface when your landscape is created. Newly created landscapes may take a few minutes to generate. If you choose a newly created landscape that is still being created, it will have an hourglass icon displayed after the landscape name; use the Refresh button to the left of the drop-down field until the hourglass is replaced by a green checkmark , indicating your new landscape is ready.
  2. (Optional) Specify an area of interest (polygon or shapefile) within an existing landscape for summary. To run a summary across the entire landscape, leave this field blank.
  3. Click the Request a Report button.
  4. A prompt appears with the name of your landscape across the top. Click Create Report to generate the Summary Report.
  5. The icon indicates the Summary Report is being generated. You can refresh and check the status by clicking the refresh button .
  6. When finished your Summary Report will be available by clicking on the report name in the confirmation dialogue box.

    7. Depending upon landscape size, your summary may take a few minutes to complete.

It may also be viewed from My Workspace, in the same folder as the landscape you chose to summarize. You may also view your report outputs in Map Studio.

Viewing Fuel Moisture Values

From My Workspace, go to the project folder containing your Landscape Summary files and click the View Summary button to open the report.

In the Summary Report, the fuel moisture information, along with the RAWS station details, may be found at the top of the report.

Creating Treatment Polygons

To simulate and evaluate the impact of treatments, we'll delineate our planned treatment areas with shapefiles in Map Studio. First by creating a polygon for each treatment area, then by combining those into one shapefile to be used in a landscape comparison later.

To begin, create three individual shape files:

  1. Open Create/Edit Shapes
  2. Select your method, in this case the freehand draw method
  3. Draw your first polygon.

    4. You can use the delete button to remove the polygon if you are not satisfied with it. In this example, draw three polygons.

  4. Give the polygon a name, in this case 'Treatment Unit 1'
  5. Specify a folder to save the polygon in: 'Haypress Creek Project'
  6. Click Create

After your first polygon is complete, repeat this process for your second and third polygons. For this example they should approximate the image below, but they do not need to be exact.

Initially your three individual polygons will be highlighted in yellow. You will want to save the three of these as one shapefile for later use in IFTDSS. To do this:

  1. Click the Copy from Existing Features button , in Create/Edit Shapes
  2. Make sure the Select multipart option is selected, then
  3. Left mouse click your first polygon, you will notice its color changes from yellow to green. This color will change back to yellow as you selected the other polygons, but the shape is still being saved.
  4. Left click on polygon two, then three.
  5. Next, type a name for your polygons in the edit box
  6. specify the folder to save it in
  7. Click Create.

When completed, your polygons will change from highlighted to a purple shaded outline on the map as shown below.

(For other projects, you may also upload shapefiles if you prefer)

Saved shapes are available in My Workspace in the folder you selected (in this case the Haypress Creek Project folder).

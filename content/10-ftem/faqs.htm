Wildfires

What if I don't see my wildfire?

Wildfire data is pulled into the FTEM system nightly from IRWIN and GEOMAC.

The most common reason a wildfire is not visible in FTEM is a lack of Control Date, Containment Date, or Out Date in the system of record.

  1. If this date remains blank, it will not become visible in FTEM until 45 days or record inactivity.
  2. An FTEM user could contact their FTEM Agency Lead to let them know the fire is not appearing, and to check for this issue in the system of record.

For more details see the FTEM Wildfire Technical Topic.

How Does my Region affect what fires I see in FTEM?

When you create your FTEM account you will be asked to specify a region. This does not limit the treatments or wildfires you see. Your region simply constrains the map extent you see when you first open FTEM. You are still free to scroll and search for fires/treatments anywhere on the FTEM map.

The list of fires and interactions you see in FTEM is determined by the map area you are zoomed into. It includes all agency fires so that fires which burn onto and through your agency lands are accounted for.

Why do I only see a point to represent my wildfire?

The wildfire Data is pulled from IRWIN for points and GeoMac for perimeters. If no perimeter has been loaded in GeoMac for your wildfire, you will only see a point representing it, regardless of the acreage. You can temporarily upload a perimeter for viewing purposes if you have one available (details in this are available in the Add shapes in FTEM Topic.)

Please see the wildfire Technical Documentation for more detail.

If you have a wildfire point and would like to view polygon data from your local machine when monitoring, Please see the FTEM Adding Shapes topic.

Why does FTEM and FIRESTAT differ?

FTEM gets fire data through IRWIN, which gathers fire data from FireCode, WildCAD, Sit/209, and WFDSS. Currently FIRESTAT, the final fire reporting system for the USFS, is "read-only" for IRWIN data, which means that changes made in FIRESTAT do not propagate back into IRWIN. Thus, fire data in FTEM may not reflect the same values seen in the FIRESTAT application. Efforts are underway to have Forest Service final fire reports reflected in IRWIN within the next year or two.

Treatments

How do I find my treatments?

Treatments in FTEM are organized by wildfire, pick your wildfire first using the date list and wildfire name search. Once you have chosen your wildfire, click the Treatments button to see a list of the treatments interactions for that fire. Use the Add or Remove Treatment buttons to refine your treatment list.

What about my region? When you create your FTEM account you will be asked to specify a region. This does not limit the treatments or wildfires you see. Rather, it helps direct your account requests to the proper FTEM Agency Administrator for your region.

What if I don't see my treatment?

Treatments are pulled into FTEM nightly from FACTS and NFPORS and must have the following attributes in the system of record:

  • For NFPORS data, the treatment must be NFP (National Fire Plan), or non-NFP.
  • For FACTS data:
    • A treatment must be KP 3 or KP 6.
    • Where duplicate SUIDs (Sub Unit ID) exist, the SUID with the most recent completion date (ActualCompletionDate) will be chosen.
    • If completion date (ActualCompletionDate) is also duplicated, treatments will be filtered by the the Treatment Category (treatment_category) in this order: 1-Fire, 2-Mechanical, 3-Other, 4-Preparation for Treatment.
    • If Treatment Category is also duplicated, treatments will be filtered by FACTS Activity Code by choosing the smallest numerical code in the remaining duplicate SUIDS.
    • If FACTS Activity Code is duplicated, no further filtering will be applied and remaining treatments will be brought in to FTEM.

If your treatment meets the above criteria but is still not visible in the FTEM Treatment list:

  1. Try increasing your search buffer for interacting treatments. This is done after you select your wildfire, but using the Add Treatments button on the Treatments tab. See the Search Buffer Topic for a step by step walk-through of how to do this. You can also add a using the Map, which is especially useful if the treatment is outside of the maximum buffer area.
  2. If the above do not resolve this issue, you may need to follow up with your FTEM Agency Administrator to have your treatment entered in the system of record. Individual agencies may upload records at different intervals.

For more details see the FTEM Treatments Technical Topic.

What if I notice a treatment is not correct or needs to be changed?

Treatments need to be correct or edited via your agency's system of record. FTEM pulls data from those records nightly.

What if not all the treatment listed for a fire need monitoring?

You can add or remove treatments from the treatment list with the Add or Remove treatment buttons.

See the treatments section of the FTEM walk-through topic for more detail on this.

What if treatments are loaded into FTEM after I've started monitoring a wildfire?

The new treatments will be available once in FTEM for users to add manually but the auto detect system will not pick them up once a fire has had monitoring started on it. So if you are missing treatments or think they have been updated in the system, you must select them on the map and add them from their or choose the add treatments button in the treatments tab to add them. We can wordsmith later.

Some of the treatments listed in the treatments Tab belong to a different unit or subunit, should I remove them?

Consult with the other unit or subunit before removing their treatments from the treatment list. When monitoring has been completed on all the treatments in the treatment list, FTEM will notify system administrators that upward reporting can now occur. A wildfire where FTEM has been completed can be amended, but any treatments removed would have to be manually added back in one at a time using the Add treatments button.

How is the treatments list automatically populated once I select a wildfire?

For wildfires with an associated perimeter, any treatment that enters that perimeter is displayed.

For wildfires represented by a point, a circle with its center at the wildfire’s point of origin is used to determine the potential treatment interactions for a wildfire. The radius of the circle is based upon calculating the length of an ellipse whose length is 1.5 times its width and whose area is equal to the size of the wildfire. The radius of the circle equals the length of the ellipse.

If a wildfire is represented by both a point and a perimeter, the FTEM application will use the perimeter buffer regardless of the buffer type, it is up to the user to determine which treatments to move to the monitor tab within the FTEM application.

For more detail you may consult the FTEM Technical Documentation Topics on buffering, treatments, and wildfires.

Treatment Interactions

My wildfire didn't interact with treatments but FTEM says it did, how do I correct it?

This question most often comes up when a wildfire shows up in FTEM as "Check for interactions" (displayed in a red color), but the person monitoring knows no treatments interacted or need monitoring.

In this case you'll need to remove the treatments so that the fire appears with "No Interactions Detected", rather than "Check for interactions". To do this:

  1. Select the wildfire.

  2. Move to the Treatments tab.

  3. Select all the treatments.

  4. Use the Remove treatments button to remove them.

How do I monitor a fire that interacts with treatments from multiple agencies?

For FTEM monitoring, each agency will enter data on their own treatments.

If you find it helpful, you may view different agency treatment layers using the Add layers widget when in the map view.

For those generating FTEM reports, the reports for all agencies will be available once the monitoring is completed.

If you need an FTEM role for more than one agency:

Request access to more than agency role from your FTEM User Profile. Each role must be approved by a FTEM Agency Lead. To see the full details on how to do this, see the drop-down on updating your User Role in the FTEM Account Topic.

If I complete monitoring and later need to update or edit, can I do that? If so, how?

A wildfire where FTEM has been completed can be updated or edited. Once you locate your fire in FTEM, go to the Complete tab and select Amend. Provide a simple justification for the change into the pop-up box and you will be able to make any necessary adjustments.

Why is the "Treatment acreage burned by wildfire" field blank for my interaction polygon?

IFTDSS will autopopulate interaction acre for a polygon treatment area, however there is a scenario in which this does not occur. If a treatment first enters the system as a point, receives some monitoring information, and then is later updated in the system as a polygon. In these cases the "Treatment acreage burned by wildfire" field will be blank. If this occurs, you may: 

  • Input the treatment interactions manually
  • Make a note of the existing monitoring data, then remove the treatment from the wildfire, and re-add the treatment to the fire. This will remove the monitoring data for the treatment, but will auto-populate the interaction acres. The monitoring data will have to be re-populated.

Policy and Data Questions

Where does FTEM data come from & where does it go?

Wildfire Data

wildfire point data are imported into FTEM via IRWIN (Integrated Reporting of Wildland-Fire Information), a service that acts as a real time conduit for fire data between the various wildland fire applications. wildfire perimeter data are pulled from GeoMAC (Geospatial Multi Agency Coordination), an application that is updated daily based upon input from incident intelligence sources. The FTEM Application is updated daily to incorporate fires reaching controlled status from Integrated Reporting of Wildland-Fire Information (IRWIN) and GeoMAC. For more information, see the FTEM Technical Documentation Topic.

Treatment Data

The FTEM process relies on geospatial Integrated Point and Polygon Fuels treatment Layers that depict completed fuels treatments from four land management Agencies within the Department of Interior (National Park Service, Bureau of Land Management, US Fish and Wildlife Service, and Bureau of Indian Affairs) as well as the US Forest Service. The FTEM Integrated Point and Polygon Fuels treatment Layers are derived from data stored in:

  • The Hazardous Fuels Reduction (HFR) Module of the National Fire Plan Operations and Reporting System (NFPORS). NFPORS is the national system for tracking fuels accomplishments for all agencies in the Department of Interior.
  • The Forest Service Activity Tracking System (FACTS). FACTS is the official system for tracking U.S. Forest Service fuels accomplishments.

For more information, see the FTEM Technical Documentation Topic.

Once you enter data into FTEM...

FTEM data is summarized and analyzed at multiple levels and across various scales to demonstrate the utility of the Hazardous Fuels Reduction program for all interested entities including, the Departments of Interior and Agriculture, Office of Management and Budget (OMB), Congress, Program Managers, Interest groups, and the public.

Is completing FTEM a requirement or optional?

Requirement. Anytime a wildfire starts in or interacts with a fuel treatment area, all agencies require that the outcome is documented to examine whether the treatment had the desired effect of reduced fire behavior and/or provided opportunities to firefighters for effective management of the wildfire (2017 Interagency Standards for Fire and Fire Aviation Operations “Red Book”). The FTEM application provides the framework for documenting these outcomes consistently across agencies in a manner that demonstrates the utility of the Hazardous Fuels Reduction program for all interested entities including, the Departments of Interior and Agriculture, Office of Management and Budget (OMB), Congress, Program Managers, Interest groups, and the public.

General

Can I upload a shapefile if my wildfire is just represented in FTEM by a point?

Yes, temporarily. You can upload shapes for viewing purposes to help you complete your monitoring, the shapes are not retained when you leave the system.

To permanently add a shapefile for a fire or treatment you'll need to update the systems of record FTEM uses (FACTS or NFPORS for treatments, IRWIN or GEOMAC for wildfires).

A treatment for 2018 has already been entered into the legacy FTEM system, do I need to enter it again here?

No. You do not need to re-enter 2018 data into the FTEM new version of IFTDSS, if you have already entered it in the legacy system.

Is data that had been loaded into FTEM in the years before 2017 included in the new system?

Data prior to 2017 is not loaded into the current version of FTEM. If you need to view that data you will have to access the legacy FTEM system and contact the agency lead for FTEM.

What file types can I upload into FTEM as attachments?

Using the Add Attachments button, users can upload MS Word documents, PDFs, JPGs, GIFs, and PNGs.

If you have a video file, that is stored in a stable location, you may include the link in a .Doc or PDF file.

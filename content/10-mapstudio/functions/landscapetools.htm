Landscape Tools

Landscape Tools enables you to view or create new landscape files from within Map Studio. The options available include:

Add an Existing Landscape to the Map

The Add to Map option lists all the available landscape files for display.

Once a landscape is chosen by selecting the checkbox next to it, it will become visible, and Map Studio will automatically zoom to that landscape.

The Filter by Map Extent option allows you to filter your available landscape by their spatial extent, this can be convenient if you have many different landscape files saved in your IFTDSS account.

The Refresh option refreshes the landscape list with the most recent files, this is convenient if you have just downloaded a landscape file but do not see it listed yet.

It may take several minutes after creating a landscape file for it to become visible in the Landscape Tools list of available landscapes.

 

Create a New Landscape

To create landscape from within Map Studio click the Landscape Tools widget along the bottom of Map Studio. In the Landscape Tools box, make sure the Create new tab is selected.

In the Create new tab specify the requirements for your landscape:

  1. Use the Map Studio tools such as zoom, basemap layers,and so forth to locate your desired area.

  2. Define the area of your new landscape by doing one of the following:

    • Selecting an Area of Interest from the drop-down menu (this contains any shapes you've created or shapefiles you've uploaded).

      Your shape or shapefile must be within your current map extent to appear in the drop-down menu, so zoom in or out in Map Studio as needed.

    • Click Select draw mode , mouse to your location on the map, hold down the left mouse key, and draw a rectangle around your desired area, releasing the button when you are finished. Clicking the black select extent button then draw a box around your area by holding down the left mouse key, drawing, and release.
    • Enter exact coordinates rather than draw a box around your area, you may do so by entering the coordinates, in degrees, for the west, east, south, and north bounds of your desired area.

    3. The maximum area for a landscape is 3.5 million acres.

  3. Choose the version of LANDFIRE data you would like to use.
  4. Choose the fire behavior fuel model (Fuel Model 40 or Fuel Model 13) to include in your landscape file.
  5. Give your landscape a descriptive name, this is helpful as you accumulate more landscape files in IFTDSS. Spaces are allowed in names and we recommend including the LANDFIRE version and keeping the name less than 30 characters. For example, "Smith Project LF14".
  6. Select the folder to store your landscape file. If needed, you may use the Add new Folder button to create a new folder for your landscape. If you do not specify an existing folder or create a new one in this step, your saved landscape defaults to the "Playground" folder of My Workspace.

  7. Click Create to generate your landscape file.

Once you click Create a message appears at the top of the Landscape Tools box to confirm your landscape is being created. It may take a few minutes to generate your landscape. You may check its status by refreshing the Layer List in Map Studio.

Tip: To refresh your map view, click the Add to map tab, followed by the Filter by Map Extent button to show only your new landscape area, and then click Refresh. A checkbox will appear next to your landscape when it is completed.

Once you click Create a message will appear at the top of the Landscape Tools box to confirm your landscape is being created. When complete, you will be able to add your new landscape to your map by clicking the Add to map tab (to the left of the Create new tab you are currently on), and selecting your newly created landscape. It will also be available in My Workspace.

It may take a couple minutes before your landscape is available for viewing. If you are unsure of your landscape status, you may switch to the Add to Map tab of Landscape Tools and use the Refresh button to update the landscape list.
